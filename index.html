Artist Introduction

Zidian Pan (ppuzzledd) is an artist currently studying on the MFA Computational Arts programme, where her work explores the intersection under the context of humanity and computation. Through her artistic practice she demonstrates the breadth of her creative expression using a variety of materials and media, including interactive installations, audiovisual performances, projections with sculptures, digital illustrations, and even the integration of web design.

Her current themes are mostly exploring her understanding of technology or using computational art as a means of expressing her thoughts on a particular topic, hoping to use the 'future archaeology' perspective as one of the directions for future art practice. At the same time, she believes that dirtiness and mystery are hidden in the fables, trying to discover the alienating and unpleasant part of the language that exists in computational art. Influenced by her background of Law, the artist subconsciously moves away from the emotions and perspectives of the people involved, based on the accumulation of basic undergraduate knowledge and practical experience, and instead explores the whole set of social logic behind these events, expressing the "untouchable reality", hoping that the work will bring out the viewer's thoughts on the logic behind social issues.
